Your full score and explanation

Listening 6.0

Test takers at Band 6 can typically listen to and process stretches of speech to pick out discrete information, and can follow detailed instructions. They can understand directly-stated facts, attitudes, opinions and purposes. They are able to pick out main ideas and infer implied meanings reasonably well. They can keep sections of what they have heard in short-term memory. They can understand vocabulary relating to familiar and some less familiar topics.

📈 How to improve: Continue learning more words on more topics. Learn common words and expressions used in talks and presentations to signal organisation. Listen regularly to authentic recordings and video clips on TV or the internet. Use subtitles to help if needed. Try to understand the relationship between different speakers' ideas.

Reading 5.5

Test takers at Band 5.5 typically deal reasonably well with straightforward factual and opinion-based texts. They are able to use vocabulary knowledge to create meaning, but mostly within sentences rather than across sentences. They can understand directly stated information, opinions and arguments, and some implied meanings. They are generally able to extract key words from texts, and have a limited ability to synthesize concrete information.

📈 How to improve: Read different types of texts, including academic ones. Use different reading strategies: decide which parts need careful reading and which can be skimmed. Make predictions based on titles. Identify clues that signal important points or the writer's attitude.

Writing 5.5

Test takers at Band 5.5 can partly answer the question. They may include too much detail and no overview. They can give their own point of view and some main ideas, although these may not lead to conclusions. They can partly organise writing and use some joining words, though sometimes wrongly. They have enough vocabulary to answer the question, although spelling mistakes may cause difficulty. They can use some basic sentences correctly and attempt complex sentences with errors.

📈 How to improve: Practice writing one-sentence summaries of your main points. Put different points in different paragraphs. Check that your writing matches the task type (letter, report, essay). After writing, combine simple sentences into complex ones. Check spelling carefully.

Speaking 5.0

Test takers at Band 5 can keep speaking but with noticeable pauses. Speech may be slow, with frequent repetition or self-correction. Basic linking of ideas exists, but ideas may be difficult to follow at times. They have enough vocabulary for familiar topics, but frequent errors occur. They rarely attempt paraphrase. Basic grammar structures are sometimes accurate, but complex structures are rare. Pronunciation problems may cause misunderstanding.

📈 How to improve: Learn vocabulary on unfamiliar topics. Write sentences using new words, then combine them into longer sentences. Read aloud and focus on pronunciation. Group words that naturally go together. Listen to English programmes to improve pronunciation and fluency.
